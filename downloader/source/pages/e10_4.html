What Does Contemporary Art Look Like Without It?

Show location info
Visual Arts

Raglan Street Gallery

Digital Outlawed is a new media exhibition that imagines a world without computers. The notable exclusion of digital media in the exhibition explores an artistic disquiet; has digital media become a criterion of contemporary art?

The Australian government defines contemporary art as 'employing new techniques and new media' and new media as 'work that uses new technologies'. With an emphasis on the new, what happens to the old; are artworks that employ old technology simply ‘un-contemporary'?

Many art mediums synonymous with new media (video, projection, sound, photography, installation, performance) existed before the digital age. Reason follows that it should be possible to create artworks in these mediums without it. This is the challenge set for the Digital Outlawed artists.

Artists: Lauren Dunn, Amy-Jo Jory, Travis John, Darcey Bella Arnold, T.R. Carter, Jake Preval & Arie Rain Glorie

Curated and Produced by Arie Rain Glorie
